74  /*
75  Computes the invariant noise budget (in bits) of a ciphertext. The invariant
76  noise budget measures the amount of room there is for the noise to grow while
77  ensuring correct decryptions. This function works only with the BFV scheme.
78 
79  @par Invariant Noise Budget
80  The invariant noise polynomial of a ciphertext is a rational coefficient
81  polynomial, such that a ciphertext decrypts correctly as long as the
82  coefficients of the invariantnoise polynomial are of absolute value less
83  than 1/2. Thus, we call the infinity-norm of the invariant noise polynomial
84  the invariant noise, and for correct decryption requireit to be less than
85  1/2. If v denotes the invariant noise, we define the invariant noise budget
86  as -log2(2v). Thus, the invariant noise budget starts from some initial
87  value, which depends on the encryption parameters, and decreases when
88  computations are performed. When the budget reaches zero, the ciphertext
89  becomes too noisy to decrypt correctly.
90 
91  @param[in] encrypted The ciphertext
92  @throws std::invalid_argument if the scheme is not BFV
93  @throws std::invalid_argument if encrypted is not valid for the encryption parameters
94  @throws std::invalid_argument if encrypted is in NTT form
95  */
